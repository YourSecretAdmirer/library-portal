About

Farah Hana binti Badrul Hisam is the Systems & Collections Manager at Auralis Library, a forward-thinking digital drama library that curates, protects, and delivers dramatic content in cutting-edge formats such as interactive virtual reality, augmented performance spaces, and AI-curated archives. With a decade of experience in digital information systems, Farah specializes in metadata architecture, long-term digital preservation, and the integration of immersive media formats into public knowledge systems.

Responsibilities

  • Digital Infrastructure Development: Farah oversees the architectural planning and system integration of digital repositories at Auralis Library. She ensures seamless interoperability across AR/VR interfaces, immersive playback systems, and multilingual archives. Her work allows global users to experience drama in various technological formats—from virtual theater to smart-script narration engines.
  • Metadata and Schema Design: She designs complex metadata schemas that allow for dynamic discovery, accessibility, and preservation of drama works. These schemas accommodate multilingual tagging, AI-driven categorization, and real-time user-generated annotations.
  • Preservation and Integrity Monitoring: Farah manages digital integrity audits and long-term archival preservation of files in both structured and experiential formats. She establishes policies to ensure data resilience against hardware decay, software obsolescence, and evolving access methods.
  • Staff Coordination and Knowledge Transfer: She trains internal staff and external partners on metadata standards, system use, AI-assisted cataloging, and rights management. She also coordinates technical workshops to keep the library team updated on global trends and protocols.
  • Analytics and Strategy: Farah leads data-driven initiatives, analyzing usage behavior, archive access trends, and global interest metrics. Her reports are key to strategic planning and resource allocation for Auralis Library's digital future.

Key Projects

  • Interactive Drama Graph: A web of interlinked dramatic works, creators, and themes built using semantic metadata, allowing researchers and users to explore relationships across cultures, languages, and genres.
  • Virtual Script Hub: A tool that enables immersive reading and performance testing of scripts through virtual environments. Readers can choose branching scenes, alternate endings, and even direct their own versions of a play.
  • Global Cinematic Archive Sync: A real-time metadata synchronization system between Auralis Library and international repositories. This project promotes collaborative drama preservation and shared access through secure distributed systems.
  • Adaptive Access Engine: A backend service that adjusts content display and interaction methods based on user preferences, language, disability access settings, and cultural context.
  • DramaLens AI: An artificial intelligence that assists in classifying new dramatic works by genre, mood, performance format, and potential audience, enhancing the discoverability of global works.
